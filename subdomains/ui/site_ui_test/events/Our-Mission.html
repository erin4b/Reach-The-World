Our Mission

Reach the World's mission is to connect volunteer world travelers with young students in disadvantaged public schools. RTW travelers are matched 1-to-1 with classrooms for a semester or an academic year. Through a highly structured, Standards-based program of web-based journalism, videoconferencing and collaborative project-making, students go on virtual journeys with their correspondents and expand their worldview in the process. RTW also provides a curriculum called GeoGames, which teaches core geographic literacy.

Most of Reach the World's travelers are studying abroad as Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholars, a competitive U.S. State Department program that provides college students with need-based scholarships to study abroad. Others are independently working, living or exploring abroad.

Reach the World is a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization that depends, in part, on core support from corporations, foundations and individuals. Earned revenue from program fees provides the balance. All contributions are tax-deductible as provided by IRS law. Proceeds from the 2011 Benefit will support the costs of RTW's online journey programs for students and teachers in under-funded public schools.
